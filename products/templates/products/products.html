Jack Fairfield was born on the 27th July 1915, in Windygates, Fife, Scotland. The oldest of four brothers, he enrolled in the Royal Army Ordinance Corps on the 16th March 1940.

During his time in the forces he wrote a journal that provides an extraordinary insight into an everyday individual who volunteered for service in WW2. His beginnings, the places he visited, the people he saw, the events he witnessed; along with his private thoughts of anguish, injustice, and happiness he experienced, are all covered throughout his journey.

Included also in this book is a culmination of extensive research, which aids to add some context and explore in more depth some of the more interesting aspects and locations Jack writes about.
